There is a forest of maker content, education etc out there. This is just a small, but expandable list of cool things some of us have seen on the internet.

## YouTube
Let's start with the best and biggest: YouTube. You can practically find anything in here, so it is quite hard to filter it out. But we tried our best:


### Hardware

[pierogi engineering](https://www.youtube.com/@pierogiengineering) - One of the best and funniest. No one loves gyroscopes more than him.

[Works By Design](https://www.youtube.com/@worksbydesign) - Very high quality maker videos, big recommendation.

[Henry Segerman](https://www.youtube.com/@henryseg) - He is a (self prescribed) mathematical maker.

[Matthew Lim](https://www.youtube.com/@matthewlim88) - Quite soothing and relaxing videos about small maker projects.

[Henry Kidman](https://www.youtube.com/@henry_kidman) - High Energy + Cinematic maker videos.

[DIYPerks](https://www.youtube.com/@DIYPerks) - Building cool stuff.

[ExcessiveOverkill](https://www.youtube.com/@ExcessiveOverkill) - He makes "cool stuff". F.e. a fire lightsaber or working on an industrial robot arm.

[HTXStudio](https://www.youtube.com/@HTXStudio) - Entertaining, high quality maker content. 

[Engineezy](https://www.youtube.com/@Engineezy) - Visually pleasing (marble machines) and robots.

[MadeByDennis](https://www.youtube.com/@MadeByDennis) - Specific crazy and cool projects.

[soiboi soft](https://www.youtube.com/@soiboisoft) - He is working on a very cool vacuum display. Big reccomendation.

[Salim Benbouziyane](https://www.youtube.com/@salimbenbouz) - Very much a maker channel, also explains his projects and uploads the project files.

[Pinouts](https://pinouts.org/) - Free pinouts PDF with more than 200 parts in extremely good quality!

#### Educational

[KnowArt](https://www.youtube.com/@KnowArt) - Educative channel about many mechanical concepts and how things work in general. Gives off "Sendung mit der Maus" vibes :).

[Inside Everything](https://www.youtube.com/@INSIDEVERYTHING1) - I mean the name says it all. He is going literally inside everything.

[Technology Connections](https://www.youtube.com/@TechnologyConnections) -  Explains how Technology (f.e. Appliances) work

[Posy](https://www.youtube.com/@PosyMusic) - Cinematic, beautiful and calm videos about tech. Definitely a recommendation.

[styropyro](https://www.youtube.com/@styropyro) - He is rather destroying than creating, but very interesting.

[MadeWithLayers](https://www.youtube.com/@MadeWithLayers) - The guy for anything 3D printing

[The Stock Pot](https://www.youtube.com/@TheStockPot-AU/videos) - Does smart home content.

[media.ccc.de](https://www.youtube.com/@mediacccde) - The ccc YouTube channel, where a subset of the talks are hardware related and quite interesting.

[Stuff Made Here](https://www.youtube.com/@StuffMadeHere) - Interesting contraptions and sciency topics

#### Electronics/Computers

[Jeff Geerling](https://www.youtube.com/@JeffGeerling) -  Mostly talking about Hardware and networking.

[Christian Lempa](https://www.youtube.com/@christianlempa) - Services for self hosting and server stuff.

[Axiometa](https://www.youtube.com/@axiometa) - Technical, high quality maker content. Mainly LED Stuff.

[bitluni](https://www.youtube.com/@bitluni) - Lots of LED matrix stuff and he built a DIY RISC-V GPU.

[GreatScott](https://www.youtube.com/@greatscottlab) - Scott tells you all about microcontrollers and embedded projects.

[This Does Not Compute](https://www.youtube.com/@ThisDoesNotCompute) - Has a lot of interesting vintage hardware projects

### Hacking

[f4mi](https://www.youtube.com/@f4micom/videos) - Less hardware, more hacky.

[Benn Jordan](https://www.youtube.com/@BennJordan) - also rather hacky. If you want to know more about the american surveillance state, this is the channel.

### Keyboards

[Cheese Turbulence](https://www.youtube.com/@CheeseTurbulence) - He is building Keyboards, mostly.

[Flurples](https://www.youtube.com/@flurples) - Flurples Law: If it can be a Keyboard, it will be a Keyboard.

[Ben Vallack](https://www.youtube.com/@BenVallack) - Crazy guy building Keyboards and endlessly optimizing his work setup

### 3D Modelling

[Deltahedra3D](https://www.youtube.com/@deltahedra3D) - CAD using FreeCAD. Good for beginners.

[Ducky3D](https://www.youtube.com/@TheDucky3D) - Motion graphics and cinematic animations in Blender

[Polyfjord](https://www.youtube.com/@Polyfjord) - Modelling, 3D tracking, photogrammetry, basic CGI effects, fluid/smoke simmulation in Blender

## Websites

### 3D Printing

[Yeggi](https://www.yeggi.com) - A 3D model aggregator for community 3D Models. No need to go to every website below (even though website specific searches work better sometimes)

[Maker World (Bambu Lab)](https://makerworld.com/en) - Bambu Labs own 3D model community.

[Printables](https://www.printables.com) - Prusas own 3D model community.

[Thingiverse](https://www.thingiverse.com) - Another 3D model community.

### Manufacturing

[JLCPCB](https://jlcpcb.com) - Here you can order PCBs, 3D Prints, CNC parts, metal sheet parts etc. ([PCBWay](https://www.pcbway.com/))

[Reichelt](https://www.reichelt.de) - Electronics store.

[Mouser Electronics](https://www.mouser.de/) - Electric components, mostly for PCBs
